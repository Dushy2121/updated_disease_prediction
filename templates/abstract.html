Abstract


Emerging infectious diseases have become one of the most pressing global health concerns in recent decades. From localized epidemics to full-blown pandemics like COVID-19, the rapid spread of infectious agents can disrupt healthcare systems, destabilize economies, and threaten lives at an unprecedented scale. One of the key challenges in combating these diseases lies in accurately predicting outbreak risks across diverse geographical regions and for a wide variety of disease types. Traditional outbreak prediction models often fall short due to several limitations. Many are disease-specific, lacking the flexibility to adapt to new pathogens or novel transmission patterns. Others struggle with real-time adaptability, relying on outdated or incomplete data that hinder timely decision-making. Furthermore, existing systems frequently ignore critical contextual factors—such as mortality rates, healthcare infrastructure, mobility patterns, and socioeconomic variables—which significantly influence the trajectory of disease spread and the effectiveness of public health responses. In this project, we propose the development of a comprehensive and intelligent disease outbreak prediction system powered by advanced machine learning (ML) techniques. The solution will employ a hybrid of transfer learning, ensemble modeling, and explainable AI (XAI) to enhance both the accuracy and interpretability of predictions. Unlike conventional approaches, our model will be designed to operate at a global scale, integrating diverse data sources such as climatic variations, population density, social media activity, and health reports from international bodies like the WHO and CDC.
