Abstract


This project focuses on designing and developing a machine learningâ€“based epidemic forecasting system capable of predicting the outbreak of infectious diseases across the globe. The scope of the system extends beyond traditional prediction models by integrating a broad range of structured and unstructured data sources to improve forecasting accuracy, real-time adaptability, and generalizability across multiple diseases and regions. The proposed system will utilize various data inputs including historical epidemiological reports, real-time health data, climate factors (such as temperature, humidity, and rainfall), human mobility patterns, social media signals, and even genomic information. These diverse datasets will enable the model to capture the complex interplay of factors contributing to disease spread, allowing for more dynamic and accurate predictions. Additionally, mobility data from public transportation APIs and crowd density analysis will help forecast region-wise transmission risks.
